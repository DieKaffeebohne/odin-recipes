Roasted Pork Shoulder with Fennel and Confit

Description

The following recipe is a slow-cook method for those who like their meats to retain plenty of moisture. The preperation of confit is a way to take advantage of the cooking fats to add a more robust flavor to the meal.

Ingredients
  • Raw Pork shoulder
  • Garlic Powder
  • Fennel
  • Salt
  • pepper
  • arrowroot
Steps
  1. Rinse pork shoulder and season with Garlic, Fennel, Salt, and Pepper
  2. Let seasoned pork shoulder sit in fridge for 24 hours
  3. Add two tbsp of olive oil or butter to cast iron pan over medium low heat
  4. Set oven to 300 degrees
  5. Raise heat to medium high, searing pork shoulder on both sides until achieving desired crisp
  6. With the fat side up, place pork shoulder in the oven, in the cast iron pan used for searing
  7. Cook shoulder in the oven for 2hrs and 45mins, checking periodically
  8. Once the shoulder is done cooking, remove from oven and let rest on cutting board
  9. Add arrowroot to the fat and oil in the pan to thicken into sauce
  10. pour confit over sliced pork and serve
